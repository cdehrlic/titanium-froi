Why an Audit

Most employers can't see where their comp costs really come from.

Workers' comp costs don't come from one place. They come from thin safety programs, slow claim reporting, weak investigations, unchallenged reserves, mod calculation errors, and fraud that nobody screened for. Your carrier isn't going to walk you through all of that; a lot of it works in the carrier's favor.

We examine your entire program the way we've done from every seat in the industry: the carrier side, the TPA side, and the employer's side. We know where the problems hide because we've sat where the decisions get made.

The Scope

One audit. The full life of a claim.

Four subject areas, examined end to end.

🦺

1 · Safety: Before the Accident

The cheapest claim is the one that never happens, and the second cheapest is the one your injured worker returns from quickly.

  • Review of your written safety programs, training records, and OSHA compliance basics
  • Hazard and injury trend analysis: where your claims actually come from, and the fixes that prevent them
  • Return-to-work and light duty program review, the single biggest lever on claim cost
🔎

2 · Investigation: When the Accident Happens

The first 48 hours decide whether a claim costs $5,000 or $150,000.

  • Claim intake and reporting lag audit (late reporting inflates claim costs by 30% or more)
  • Investigation protocol review: witness statements, scene evidence, video preservation, chain of custody
  • Fraud screening by a team that has identified and dismantled organized workers' comp fraud operations
🗂️

3 · Carrier Oversight & Reserves: After the Claim Is Filed

Nobody watches the people watching your claims. We do.

  • Line-by-line reserve review on every open claim; reserves quietly drive your mod and your premium
  • Adjuster accountability: are your claims being actively worked toward resolution, or just aging?
  • Litigation strategy and defense counsel spend review
📊

4 · Loss Run & Mod Review: The Scoreboard

Your experience mod is a number someone else calculated. We check their math.

  • Full loss run analysis: frequency, severity, and cost trends across your claim history
  • Mod recalculation and verification from unit stat data (carrier reporting errors are common)
  • Class code and premium audit: misclassification is a common source of overcharges
  • Forward mod projection: what each open claim could cost you over the next one to two years
